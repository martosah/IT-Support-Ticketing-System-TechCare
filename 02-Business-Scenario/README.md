
# Section 2 — Business Scenario

## The Brief

> *TechCare Solutions receives 200+ IT support requests weekly via email and phone. No centralised system exists, leading to missed SLAs, duplicated work and frustrated staff.*

The brief is short, but the scenario it describes carries real weight. Three stated pain points, several unstated ones, and a regulated context that raises the bar for the design.

---

## TechCare's Profile

- **Workforce:** 500–2,000 employees
- **Industry:** Regulated (financial services, healthcare, or similar)
- **Footprint:** Multiple offices and branches
- **IT function:** Operational but informal — staff exist and do support work, but governance and discipline are absent
- **Compliance posture:** Subject to regulatory oversight (data protection, audit, sector-specific frameworks)

The size and regulated nature of the organisation matter because they disqualify simple solutions. A 50-person startup can run IT support out of a Slack channel; a 1,500-person regulated enterprise cannot. Auditors will eventually ask for the trail of any ticket — and the answer cannot be "check someone's inbox."

---

## The Three Stated Pain Points

**Missed SLAs.** TechCare has no documented commitments, no system to start a clock at ticket creation, and no proactive alerts. SLAs cannot be measured against, breaches go unnoticed, and there is no escalation path when an agent is overloaded.

**Duplicated work.** Tickets live in personal inboxes. The same issue arrives via email and phone, and two agents work it independently. A network outage affecting 35 people produces 35 separate tickets with no link between them.

**Frustrated staff.** End users have no visibility into ticket status and chase IT for updates. Agents have uneven workloads, no clear ownership, and no way to capture or share institutional knowledge. Both populations are unhappy for different reasons.

---

## Hidden Pain Points

These aren't named in the brief but are almost certainly present at this organisational scale:

- **No regulatory audit readiness.** A regulated enterprise without an auditable IT support trail carries real exposure.
- **No data for capacity planning.** Without ticket volumes, IT leadership cannot justify hiring or tooling decisions.
- **No service catalog.** End users don't know what they can request or how to request it.
- **Knowledge concentration risk.** Critical institutional knowledge sits in a few agents' heads.
- **No improvement loop.** Without ticket data, recurring issues are never identified, and the same problems keep happening.

I designed for these alongside the stated pain points.

---

## What the Solution Must Deliver

From this analysis, the solution must provide four things:

- **Centralisation** — one system, one source of truth across all intake channels
- **Standardisation** — consistent process, classification, and prioritisation for every ticket
- **Visibility** — real-time and historical insight for agents, managers, leadership, and auditors
- **Accountability** — clear ownership, traceable actions, measurable outcomes

Every requirement in Section 5 supports at least one of these.
