
# Section 4 — Key Stakeholders

Stakeholders are the parties with an interest in the project's success or outcome — not just the people who use the system. Some are operational users; others exercise oversight or set the regulatory baseline without ever logging in.

I classified each stakeholder using the **Power/Interest Grid** to drive engagement priority, and grouped them into three categories: Internal Operational, Internal Governance, and External.

---

## Internal Operational

TechCare employees directly involved in or affected by IT service delivery.

**Chief Information Officer (CIO).** Project sponsor. Cares about strategic alignment, ROI, risk reduction, and headline service metrics. *High Power, High Interest.*

**IT Support Manager.** Primary business owner of the operational solution. Cares about team productivity, SLA compliance, and visibility into workload. *High Power, High Interest.*

**Service Desk Analysts and Specialist Technicians.** Daily users of the system. Care about efficient tooling, fair workload distribution, and recognition. *Low Power individually, High Interest collectively.*

**End Users.** TechCare employees who submit tickets. Care about fast resolution, status visibility, and easy submission. *Low Power individually, High Interest collectively.*

**Department Heads.** Care about their teams' productivity and visibility into IT issues affecting their unit. *Medium Power, Medium Interest.*

---

## Internal Governance

TechCare functions whose interest is oversight rather than operational use.

**Compliance Officer / Data Protection Officer.** Cares about audit trail completeness, retention policy, access controls, and personal data handling. *High Power, Medium Interest.*

**Internal Auditor.** Cares about demonstrable controls, audit log integrity, and segregation of duties. *High Power, Low Interest day-to-day.*

**Information Security Team.** Cares about authentication, sensitive data handling, integration security, and admin action monitoring. *High Power, Medium Interest.*

**Finance.** Cares about total cost of ownership, predictability, and justified investment. *High Power, Low Interest.*

**Human Resources.** Cares about smooth IT setup for new hires and reliable access removal for leavers. *Medium Power, Medium Interest.*

**Executive Leadership (CEO, COO, other C-suite).** Cares about operational efficiency, risk reduction, and employee experience. *High Power, Low Interest day-to-day.*

---

## External

Parties outside TechCare with legitimate interest in or interaction with the service.

**Microsoft.** Platform provider. *Low Power, Low Interest.*

**Third-Party Vendors.** External providers delivering elements of TechCare's IT services. *Low Power, Medium Interest.*

**External Auditors.** Periodic independent review of controls. *High Power, Low Interest day-to-day.*

**Regulators.** Sector-specific oversight bodies. Set the compliance baseline the design must meet. *Very High Power, Low Interest day-to-day.*
